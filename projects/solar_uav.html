Integrated GIS & UAV-Based Rooftop Solar Estimation

Kolik Village, Maharashtra Team Project Shivaji University

Project Overview

To address growing energy demands and limited electricity access in rural areas, this project utilized an integrated Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) approach. The goal was to assess rooftop solar energy potential and conduct a detailed demand-supply analysis for rural sustainability in Kolik village.

Figure 1: High-resolution (2.48cm/pixel) Orthomosaic of Kolik village generated using UAV imagery.

Methodology & Execution

  • Drone Data Acquisition: Operated a DJI Phantom 4 Pro to capture high-resolution imagery, generating Orthomosaics (2.48cm/pixel) and Digital Surface Models (DSMs) using Pix4D Mapper.
  • Feature Extraction: Digitized building rooftops to precisely quantify available surface area for solar panel installation.
  • Solar Radiation Analysis: Leveraged ArcGIS Pro tools to incorporate seasonal variations and local shading effects into energy generation capacity estimates.
  • Socio-Economic Integration: Conducted a Demand-Supply Analysis by mapping household-level electricity consumption patterns against the geospatial solar potential.
